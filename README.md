## ğŸ§  Key Insights
1. **Attrition Rate:** 16.1% of the total 1,470 employees have left the company.  
2. **Age Factor:** The highest attrition occurs among employees aged **26â€“35 years**, indicating mid-career transitions or job dissatisfaction.  
3. **Salary Slab:** Employees earning **below 5k** experience the highest attrition, highlighting the importance of **compensation satisfaction**.  
4. **Education Background:** Majority of attrition is seen in employees from **Life Sciences (38%)** and **Medical (27%)** backgrounds.  
5. **Job Role:** The **Laboratory Technician**, **Sales Executive**, and **Research Scientist** roles show the most attrition.  
6. **Years at Company:** Most employees leave **within the first year**, showing a need for better onboarding and engagement programs.  
7. **Gender-wise Attrition:** Males show slightly higher attrition compared to females.  
8. **Job Satisfaction:** Roles like **Research Scientist** and **Laboratory Technician** show varying satisfaction levels, suggesting targeted HR interventions.
